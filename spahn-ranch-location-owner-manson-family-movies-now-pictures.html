Spahn Ranch: location, owner, Manson family, movies, now pictures

Spahn Ranch is a suitable getaway spot. This idyllic retreat once served as a Western movie set. However, it is best known for playing host to Charles Manson and his followers. The Manson family clan gained notoriety for plotting the murder of the Hollywood actress Sharon Tate and others in 1969.

The mention of Spahn Movie Ranch sends shivers to the spines of most Americans. It is associated with one of the most notorious real-life crimes ever committed in the history of the country. The destination has an old Western-themed set, boulder-strewn scenery, and mountainous terrain. This makes it a suitable site for filming Western movies and TV series.

Spahn Ranch owner

George Spahn owned the infamous ranch. He was born in Chestnut Hill, Pennsylvania, on the 11th of February, 1889. He purchased the property in 1953 and changed it to bear his last name. George Spahn was married to Martha Greenholts, and they had 11 children together.

Spahn Ranch location

The Spahn Ranch address is 12000 Santa Susana Pass Road, Chatsworth, Los Angeles, California. The place is now Santa Susana Pass State Historic Park, California. Looking at the Spahn Ranch map shows that the isolated and run-down property was 55-acres in size. Furthermore, the movies ranch became a breeding ground for many stragglers, including the Manson family cult, between 1968 and 1969.

Spahn Ranch Manson family

The Manson family was a close-knitted clan and cult founded in 1960 by Charles Manson. They were active in the State of California in the late 60s and early 70s. The Manson clan was made of about 50 persons who were followers of Charles.

The family was known to live an unconventional lifestyle with the constant use of drugs like LSD. So, where was the Manson ranch? The family set their headquarters at the Spahn Ranch in the late 1960s.

How did Manson get Spahn Ranch?

Before the Manson family found shelter in the Spahn Ranch, they lived in Dennis Wilson's cabin of the Beach Boys fame. The convicted murderer and cult leader alongside his followers were evicted from the property in August 1968. Nevertheless, the Manson clan relocated to the Los Angeles County ranch after some members of the family made an acquaintance with the property owner, George Spahn.

When the Mansons arrived at the ranch in 1968, George Spahn was nearly 80 years old. He was lonely and almost a blind man who needed companionship. Spahn allowed the Mansons to live on his ranch in return for companionship and labour.

Frequently asked questions

There are lots of interesting questions that have been raised over time concerning the activities on the property and its owner. Here are some of those questions and the best answers to them so far.

What happened at Spahn Ranch?

This is where the Manson clan lived when they murdered Sharon Tate and six others in 1969. That aside, Charles Manson and his followers equally lived on Barker Ranch, which is in the northern Mojave Desert.

Does the Spahn Ranch still exist?

No. A mysterious wildfire destroyed the ranch film sets and buildings on the 26th of September, 1970. There were plans to set up a “European Tourist Center” on the property, but it did not materialise. Presently, most of the overgrown land is owned by the state.

What happened to the owner of the Spahn Ranch?

After the property burnt down, the Spahn Ranch owner returned to North Hollywood, where he lived with his wife. Then, on the 22nd of September, 1974, he died at Sherwood Convalescent Hospital in Van Nuys, Los Angeles, California. His death was due to natural causes. He was 85 years old.

Spahn Ranch is now part of California's Santa Susana Pass State Historic Park. However, it is usually remembered for being the primary residence of Charles Manson, the convicted leader of the infamous Manson cult.
